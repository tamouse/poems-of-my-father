Poems of My Father

Wrench

Gees, Mister, you didn’t take much time to fix the car. How long, would you say? The mechanic thought about the years of schooling and a childhood around garages. His tools, the hoist, the smells of oil, little cuts that never quite bled and never quite healed, and black, always the black lines on him. “How long? Oh, about two arms and a wrench.”

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

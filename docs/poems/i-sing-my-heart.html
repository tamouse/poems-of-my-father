Poems of My Father

I Sing My Heart

I sing my heart out in a wilderness like a victim of some violence too horrible to tell. My private fate that will not let itself be known until –on this calm afternoon of blind distress– a final mistic ignorance descends while those more competent than I await to sing much louder their soft vesper hymn. I listen for a shushing antinym.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

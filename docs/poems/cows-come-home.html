Poems of My Father

Cows Come Home

the shoe is stained the jacket torn the fingers aging into statue

a church tune nasals in his head having lost crescendo droning

he beats his hands together stammering a pitiful condition begging: cuppa cuppa

unless one listens back thirty years to hear the lost crescendo he claps his hands to drive the dog

cuppa cuppa .. cuh baw cuh baw “come boss, come boss” in words as old as languages old beyond all parthenons

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

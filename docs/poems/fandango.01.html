Poems of My Father

fandango

Say then how can I fly like an eagle and become quite at home in the clouds I’d like to be soaring it never was boring to be greatly admired by crowds Oh you never can fly like an eagle or become quite at home in the clouds

We have shown how to peal a potato how dumb neckties really are we wonder what next thing will find you perplexed. You really keep going too far. Oh, one additional favor I’m asking: Is this how to shine like a star?

Is this how to dance the fandango with knees almost bumping the heart magnified leaps almost dangerous sweeps oh I feel I am coming apart this is not how to dance the fandango it’s not hard if you know how to start

So is this how to dance the fandango with feather and cymbals and drums I find this intriguing although so fatiguing I just let myself flop as it comes This is not how to dance the fandango you will crash if you happen to sneeze.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

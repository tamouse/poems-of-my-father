Poems of My Father

Under Laughing Gas

Closed closed and locked brick monuments together make a city

Her buddy don’t rattle that door might set off the alarm

Closed closed and locked even the church

Hey buddy this is half-past one service is over

Closed the river cave blocked by boulders

Her buddy don’t move that stone someone might get in there

Knoll of pines opens invites place to listen

Slope of sumac hiding black currants red currants permits, preovides

Shoe moves shoe on a kid hiding in bushes ashes dropping through an open shirt

Sneak out here and burn burn his nature burn my nature chimney it through lungs

Cluster of monuments city of locks chimneys of death

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

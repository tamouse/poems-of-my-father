Poems of My Father

cobweb

A cobweb heavy with the dust it filters from the air disgraceful object, yet nobody sweeps it down, others like it hide sharp corners of the room.

Under one such cobweb-blanket on the woodbox beside the seperator, something there? We lift the web, surprised to find a souvenir, a statue we had given him one birthday.

Had he kept it here to be reminded in the morning while he took the cream for income from the milk, so as he worked the handle round and round his eyes could steady on this prize, or had he placed it here and then forgot that it was here, or if he saw failed to understand, did not remember anything of that story he had told us many times in our believing years.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

Poems of My Father

Before His Birth

how could she have known that in the days before his birth the stars converged to grow a brightness meant to let some shepherds know what stars were doing: concentrating rays as but symbolic for a larger phase of new ideas knotting to let glow–historical significance–to throw life’s meaning backward with a craze for taking god so bloody big in ways that numbed the brain; that thwarted mental flow –imagined god above and life below. The separation crosses and betrays the hope of once discovering the tie; the one of grass, plain ground with clear blue sky

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

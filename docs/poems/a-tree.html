Poems of My Father

A Tree

A tree pretends to be symmetrical The tree pretends that between each root each twig a magic straw of sweets and waters makes it possible to draw from mushroom roots and cold worm trails the happy efflorescence of an orange or a pear

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

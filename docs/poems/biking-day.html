Poems of My Father

Biking Day

One baking day on county roads when bikng was admenture, we made it to the tower on the mountain; walking mostly, pushing those strong bikes with new departure coaster brakes up that painful road to the lookout tower,

the road mocked now by cable rides and ski runs for people nobody knows to use the mountain which has for them the proper conformation: See Dick Ski

That’s how it’s gone for most of us, conformation is the use of us of most of us,

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

Poems of My Father

Cruciform

So many were with vague or sure intent discovering America, those wise lost priests, and Portuguese in fishing guise, Vikings…but not until Columbus sent back Spanish Isabel word where he went did Europe open its astonished eyes. So end of life from flood or plague was prize man lived with, but the Bomb new terror meant:

This world is ours, ours to destroy, our loss. So each one must be savior: this leaves no choice at all, must drag a bark-rough cross to die abandoned pegged between two thieves; in that last surge of life, remember moss for water’s taste with each stab he receives.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

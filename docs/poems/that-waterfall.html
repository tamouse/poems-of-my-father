Poems of My Father

That Waterfall

That waterfall, its energy, such waste, so profligate our earth is, so much time to spin around, a top, a toy, so blue and green, great clumps of grey and white and years: a hundred thousand for a cliff to break off one small bit of rock; the canyon grows by inches – millimeters then – refaced with fresher elements. God only knows how long a planetoid may take to form a half-way stable cliff that shears away thin layers, how long to see it through, change habit, heap up mountains, do earthquakes, add thought, become aware of jokes and fears, format a word, within a mind, for rhyme.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

Poems of My Father

Two Quatrains

All day my spirit picks up bits of paper on the street. At night each candy wrapper fits a dreaming; fruited, sweet.

My life may be your book, my dear; or, then again, one word left chancily upon an ear that listened, chose, and heard.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

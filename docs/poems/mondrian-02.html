Poems of My Father

Mondrian II

flying has moments of sheer delight swoops sweeps that carry a heart out of a body

my wings seemed hardly part of me diving to soar again driving against the air

then a moment of discovery a squared-off bit of sky

strange double overlapping world

I drove that ended it the crash the fall the hurt

all summer sunsets climaxed to one break

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

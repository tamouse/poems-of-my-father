Poems of My Father

Honors

he foremost among the teammates set up plays watched for the breaks the new surprising ways to fake and bring the ball down fast and score not necessarily himself looked more to find how someone else could bear the praise just as in class he wanted some good A’s to go to someone else, a mind that pays back what is lent out, for everyone, or for most when that new kid with his unblinking gaze the one who understands first and obeys was hurt for being odd, he traded: wore his shirt–symbolic–stood by, took the floor supporting him, against the easy haze foremost

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

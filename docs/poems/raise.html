Poems of My Father

Raise

I have raised my hand no reason no one is asking for an answer it is all figured out no questions come along

my hand is embarassed but muy arm needs to hold it high not to get called on not to go to the bathroom the position relieves the muddied pools of his badness

it is my arm that is raised my arm as strong as the will power to ignore what is going on

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

Poems of My Father

Solution

A sharp stinging flavor, a cane, a very large string, a floor of a ship, the buried line of a precious metal:

He agonizes over the remaining clues to the Sunday crossword puzzle. Suddenly he stands up as the tart odor of fresh blueberry pie reaches his nostrils. He grabs his walking stick, swing the honors ‘rope’ over his shoulder, ignors the solitaire deck strewed behind him, and begins the grand march, not feeling the weak spot in his aorta gives way in the last struggle of the artery to hold itself together

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

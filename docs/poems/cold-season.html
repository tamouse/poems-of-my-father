Poems of My Father

Cold Season

Drifts of winter cover roof and window, wolves surround to choir moonlit glory, human in their hunger and their hunt. Huddled where we are, inside dark snow rooms frightened; we are their game; they are not ours.

Then summer, dark and burden melt to tasks we fall to; we are ever healthy to that with the sun building harvest from our working and our dirt.

In joy we turn to waste all we have won except at night the grey beasts watch to howl us winter comes again.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

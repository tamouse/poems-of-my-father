Poems of My Father

Wordnes

A game of Scrabble takes the afternoon; That part of it I spend with you, or you With me: between the bookcase – finished soon – And watching “As You Like It,” channel two. Such letters, where one drownes, it so enwords Me. Where to play: the puzzle still remains. Snowred? A proper noun? I try ownerds– That means those weirdos who would play with pain. I’m taxing even your calm. You forebear, So I try downers. You say that’s too sad. Resowned you tell me takes a U. Such care To spell; like, “Have I onswered…” That’s so bad, You say. So Sownder? All these blunders; Will they never cease, one wonders… Wonders!

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

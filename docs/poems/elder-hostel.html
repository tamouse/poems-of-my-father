Poems of My Father

Elder Hostel

Sunset after all day blended light has hidden gold, strong yellow, orange red shatters whiteness on the crest of night hot colors blazing on their own death bed

autumn crowned in universal green a neutral hiding flashings in the leaves. consumed to show so lavish but unseen rich colors day of cooling strength receives

lifetime, sober, dutifully spent and hiding brightness, now from structure intoxicated, what had old rules meant espressions flung, sky falling fires born

the seasoned systems work with age-free prime true colors that have been there all the time.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

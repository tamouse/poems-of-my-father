Poems of My Father

Ideal Rider

I’ll tell you, ideal reader, of your future

You walked in me when I was fog and freezing you stayed when I was glacier-lost but strong you moved with me when I was spring and fountain when I raged breaking maelstromed over cliffsides you clung, you clambered through me inside backwashed caves and courses. Know then we meander natural but tame our bedway smoother every turn past marsh and grassland… wait, the tide-bore churns, new channels are cut out through the blinded delta we will see before we finish we respond to moon and sun

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

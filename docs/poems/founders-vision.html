Poems of My Father

Founders' Vision

The pathway leads beside the river where brambles tear the ground where water seeps makes each step mindless of its own direction

somehow I move against the river to see far off across a grid of thorny branches the feeding ponds with tended poles from which the lamps of Eden shine a music for the families who work the rafted gardens.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

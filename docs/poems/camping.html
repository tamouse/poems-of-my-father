Poems of My Father

Camping

I walk the trail a furrow-length into the woods

a birch tree fourteen thumbs across at one knee-height a single touch-me-not golden under clouds

a popple the top half hanging from a stump my reach and half my reach again high I look closer it is Balm of Gilead

I brush a fern a plastic drinking cup

I am one-eighth of a mile out of town

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

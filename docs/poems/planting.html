Poems of My Father

Planting

I live within a garden not my own, Yet mine to care for, see it grow and feed My nature as I tend it, flower and weed The same. Upon this path I place a foot To visit cabbages and thistles grown Precisely here, to know them head and root, To hear that rain has washed, feel sun has shone. I walk a nursing ground where I can put Myself to find a soil for flesh and bone.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

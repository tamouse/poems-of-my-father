Poems of My Father

About Nothing

when I was six and reading aloud to the class and to the teacher to prove I was there

the word NOTHING lay upon the page with no clue within its sentence within its context with no way to pronounce: No THING sure to be a snicker

yes, that was what it meant

then the teacher anxious to cut short the silence rowdy-type students are eager to fill

gave it to me NUTHING

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

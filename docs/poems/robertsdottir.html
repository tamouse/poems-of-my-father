Poems of My Father

Robertsdottir

I’d like to go back to the good old days when we were together two friends but nothing so delicate ever stays ephemeral means it soon ends

those days were friendly and sporty those days were happy and fine when I was a young lad of forty and you were a lady of nine

we went fishing but there was no nibble our bobbers just floated around but we found no reason to quibble we examined a rock we had found

and a butterfly wing and a dorty then back to the cabin to dine when I was a young lad of forty and you were a lady of nine

we built a campfire for cooking but couldn’t find very much wood so there we were I somehow looking so stupid right there where I stood

Written for Robert's daughter, Ruth
Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

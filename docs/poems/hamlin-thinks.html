Poems of My Father

Hamlin Thinks

Hamlin thinks of walking on the mountain

a hike is good an exercise worthwhile

on many levels find what flowers hide

among old rocks changing elevation

comes with new varietlies

from his eastward window he can see sunshine on the mountains It is a puzzle

light shapes here and dark tones side by side

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

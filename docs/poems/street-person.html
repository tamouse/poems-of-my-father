Poems of My Father

Street Person

under those sweet rags one with narrative legs who has vomited stars crossing and crossing the lanes to safe homesteads breaking the bars of the polite avenne hear your friends have scurried with words in the corners of their eyes under lights which burn tears they do not end with counting themselves each other give back what is no longer in them to see again the harvest in the green blade tremble teach the three-month pregnancy of foodstuff love the hollow in them their stomach and their soul unfed they neither drown nor swim in the currents of their commerce nor sleep on the mattresses of fruitful bearing nor stop in their dreams until all nights are one they say what fire dare not say then die with having been the voice of all.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

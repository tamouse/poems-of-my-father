Poems of My Father

The Voyager

The voyager returns to haunting sounds of someone cutting trees the nasty snarl that slices daytime far too thin

between drugged glory and the saw-sharp breeze he may help us see the fix we are in.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

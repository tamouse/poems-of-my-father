Poems of My Father

Green Park

Children are a river flowing out of night into a park to worship fountains. Soon they leave life’s wishing hours self-shaping, seven-skilled: a wave Through life from life, moving as form does. The grave and aging ocean, through half-waking shocks must ask: is this the soul I had… Or was?

Butchers of beauty on diets of delight they play upon green banks, their parents strewn with sandwiches and grass. They clamor noon, fast-moving and strong-willed, a raft is broken on their rocks. They laugh while killing flowers which die for them fulfilled.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

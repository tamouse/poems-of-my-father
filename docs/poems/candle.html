Poems of My Father

Candle

Have you seen my candle? Yes, it’s part of the exhibit. I don’t want to tell you how many hours… what do you think…

Really? You have a good eye.

Um, Ma’am… my candle. That one. I’d be embarrassed to admit how many hours…

Oriental influence, you think? Concentricity with justaposition in the petrolocity of wax…

All that, in my candle

I honor your opinion, madam.

So I went home I tried again

No empathy, you said.

So… years. But, now, a final form avoiding flaw

but you find a new direction I could not have known

I will bring it in again Time and again

Since i have found myself to be a candlemaker

what else have I to do

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

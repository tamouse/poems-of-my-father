Poems of My Father

Invitation From a Friend

Would you know me?

Then come walking, hold my elbow, watch my eyes, what I look at, maybe see.

Understand now, this is tricky, would you riddle with a robin?

My own flights are far more sudden.

When you know where I will go next, why the pond to me will matter, why the stars will wake me coldly, how the grass will bed me kindly, how the trees will hold me straighter, when the rain will clean my body needing washing smile to footstep, then we have begun a knowing for a woodland walk together.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

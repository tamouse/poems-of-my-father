Poems of My Father

Cyrano de Bergerac

You are still with us – artists near and far remember and respond to your staged call, between panache and rhyme contestants spar win honor’s victories – unworthies fall. While Montfleury remains the sad buffoon the count returns to make his late adieu. Now to the last of those who would lampoon the fool – extol the true – add Dépardieu. Through many characters his skills extend. The role’s magnificence is why we came, courageous acts have brought us to attend upon this presentation’s fresh acclaim. How fortunate for worlds of life and show Rostand picked up your old book, Cyrano.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

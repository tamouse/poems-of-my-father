Poems of My Father

Streams of Conscious

Rains storm down punch the grass

moles and hole dwellers cower in cold wait

a wash upon the street stones cluster downhill

somewhere along a valley ox-bows writhe

somewhere further beyond perceived dimensions a wholeness takes in all this pulling and shoving with a surface calm

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

Poems of My Father

Following Fire

After the fires, the tourists came Hoping the camps would be the same But the trees were gone. The bear And deer moved to higher ground. Everywhere Were the scars of flame.

We’d lost–we thought–what has been ours– The great pine groves, the cedar towers where a million people came each year– Wiped out–whole mountainsides burned clear. Then came the flowers.

Read during Robert's memorial by grand-daughter Chelsea
Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

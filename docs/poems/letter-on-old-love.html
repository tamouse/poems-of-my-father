Letter on Old Love

dear lover know in closing if you must in eagerness or carelessness consume

our poorest warmth this open daylight field

of our later years bares far too strong a yield please take me back into the smaller room

our first home dingy hopeful in its dusk

before weâ€™d grown our garden of distrust

no cable car for us those shaky rides

a slower surer way to race the tides

feel land-edge tremble from great ocean swells

along the star-fished beaches hand in hand

become sea creatures burrow in wet sand

find driftwood shining pebbles and old shells

be eaten where our giant playmate hides

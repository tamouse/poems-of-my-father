Poems of My Father

Rooms

as if each room of the large school was a poem or part of a poem students are assigned by age or number of rooms they have passed through which is called their grade

where they go to earn a grade grades pile up to make a school which they hope to make their way through sometimes by memorizing a poem which is also divided into rooms especially if from a forgotten age

so each year is an age and each year is a grade which means so many rooms the total of which is school of which no one writes a poem not even when they are through

it is a blast, a ball, the way through watching your own increasing age life is a poem, for some an uphill grade they cannot finish school they get lost in the rooms

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

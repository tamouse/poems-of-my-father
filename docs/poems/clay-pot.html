Poems of My Father

Clay Pot

Where will we be my friends, when someone tells us that if we are more than ordinarily intelligent lucky devoted good with our hands we may within a lifetime produce a pot as good as a thousand pots buried under ten thousand years of village dust.

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)

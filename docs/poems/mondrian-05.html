Poems of My Father

Mondrian V

bird at my window crashing and gone leaving me nothing but cracks across my outside view

one line horizons my space two others rising from a conmon point draw me outward infinite a fourth is a meander downward

on the ground the bird struggles death or long suffering

leaves me decision how to be merciful with this miracle

Poetry of Robert Lyle Temple, 1928-2018 (oldbob, dad, pa, uncle bob, popka, grampa bob, the professor)
